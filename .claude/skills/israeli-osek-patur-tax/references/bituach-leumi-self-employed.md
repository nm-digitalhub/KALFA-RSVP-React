# National Insurance (ביטוח לאומי) for the self-employed — verified reference (2026)

Fetched LIVE from btl.gov.il on 2026-07-18 (figures marked effective
01.01.2026). See `../evidence.json`. Amounts update every January.

## Who is a "עובד עצמאי" (any ONE of):

- ≥ 20 average weekly hours in the occupation; OR
- average monthly income ≥ 50% of the average wage = **6,885 ₪** (2026); OR
- ≥ 12 weekly hours AND income ≥ 15% of the average wage = **2,065 ₪** (2026).

Below all thresholds → "עצמאי שאינו עונה להגדרה": treated as a person with
non-employment income "לעניין תשלום דמי ביטוח וזכאות לקצבאות" (live quote; BTL
circular 1468). The commonly-stated consequence — not insured for work injury
(פגיעה בעבודה) — is a structural inference, not a quoted primary sentence.

## 2026 contribution rates (self-employed)

| Monthly income slice | ביטוח לאומי | בריאות | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| up to 7,703 ₪ | 4.47% | 3.23% | **7.7%** |
| 7,703 ₪ → ceiling 51,910 ₪ | 12.83% | 5.17% | **18%** |

- Minimum income floor: **3,442 ₪/month** (2026) — below it, contributions are
  computed as if earning the floor. Average wage 2026: **13,769 ₪/month**.
- Obligations: open a self-employed file at BTL immediately when activity
  starts (since 2026 a combined digital Tax-Authority+BTL process exists);
  מקדמות set from declared expected income; **correction (תיקון מקדמות) only
  if income changed ≥10%, once per quarter**; final liability reconciled from
  the income-tax assessment (הפרשי שומה).
- 52% of paid BTL contributions is income-tax-deductible (§47א) — but NOT
  under the עסק-זעיר regime (§87ד excludes it).

## Combined שכיר + עצמאי

Incomes accumulate for rates and for the insured-income ceiling; the salaried
income is accounted first (employer deducts at source), self-employed income
stacks on top. Exact computation per the btl.gov.il "עיסוקים מעורבים" page —
consult it live for a concrete case.
