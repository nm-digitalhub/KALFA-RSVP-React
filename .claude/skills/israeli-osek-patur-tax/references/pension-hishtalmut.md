# Mandatory pension + קרן השתלמות for the self-employed — verified reference (2026)

Verified 2026-07-18 via Kol Zchut (Wayback snapshots 2026-02-28 / 2026-03-12,
figures explicitly "נכון ל-2026") cross-checked with live 03/2026 sources.
2026 ceilings are frozen at 2025 levels. See `../evidence.json`.

## Mandatory pension (since 2017)

- Deposit rates on taxable income:
  - **4.45%** on income up to half the average annual wage — first **82,614 ₪**
    (2026);
  - **12.55%** from there up to the average annual wage — **165,228 ₪** (2026);
  - nothing mandatory above (voluntary allowed).
- Deposit deadline: by the END of the tax year for that year.
- Enforcement: self-employed with annual income above **74,972 ₪** (2026) who
  did not deposit get a warning; failure to deposit within 90 days → **500 ₪**
  fine (index-updated).
- Tax benefits for pension deposits (ניכוי/זיכוי) exist beyond the mandatory
  minimum — exact optimization is a CPA-question.

## קרן השתלמות (self-employed)

- Deductible as an expense (§17(5א) — **preserved even under the עסק-זעיר
  regime**): up to **4.5%** of taxable income and up to **13,203 ₪/year**
  (2026); qualifying income ceiling **293,397 ₪** (2026).
- Capital-gains-exempt deposits: up to **20,566 ₪/year** (2026).
- Exempt withdrawal after 6 years of seniority (3 years for
  study purposes / retirement age).
- Deposits above the caps are allowed but get no tax benefit / no CGT
  exemption on the excess.

## Practical annual checklist (KALFA owner)

1. By December: compute taxable income estimate → deposit mandatory pension
   (4.45%/12.55% slices) and hishtalmut up to 4.5% (≤13,203 ₪) if beneficial.
2. Verify BTL advances match actual income to avoid הפרשי שומה surprises.
3. Both interact with the עסק-זעיר 30% choice — hishtalmut stays deductible,
   BTL §47א deduction does not. Compute jointly, or route to the CPA.
